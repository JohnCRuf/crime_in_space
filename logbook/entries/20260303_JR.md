### Research Design Note - SDI Patrol Shifts as Identification for Crime Avoidance

**Date:** 2026-03-03

This note outlines an identification strategy using CPD's Strategic Decision
Initiative (SDI, "scarecrow" patrol shifts) to estimate crime-avoidance
parameters in route choice. The central idea is to use plausibly exogenous
changes in patrol presence by place and watch to instrument perceived safety
along connected Chicago routes.

---

#### What SDI Is (with Sources)

CPD describes SDI as a high-visibility patrol initiative created in
February 2022 to curb public violence citywide. In CPD's 2022 Annual Report,
SDI is described as overtime-enabled deployment (including vehicular patrol,
foot patrol, CTA premise checks, and blue-light missions), with personnel
assignable anywhere in the city based on crime patterns. The same report notes
expansion in July 2022 to seven days per week and all watches.

Public reporting and statements from CPD leadership indicate SDI (often called
"scarecrow policing") was suspended by Superintendent Larry Snelling effective
Thursday, February 1, 2024, after a suspension announcement on January 30, 2024.

Source links:
1. CPD 2022 Annual Report (Strategic Deployment Initiative description):
   https://www.chicagopolice.org/wp-content/uploads/legacy/2022-Annual-Report-FOR-PUBLICATION.pdf
2. CBS Chicago (Jan 30, 2024) on SDI suspension ending Feb 1, 2024:
   https://www.cbsnews.com/chicago/news/chicago-police-to-end-scarecrow-policing-tactic-using-parked-squad-cars-downtown/
3. WTTW (Jun 6, 2024) summary of SDI timeline and overtime use:
   https://news.wttw.com/2024/06/06/chicago-spent-more-1-million-overtime-hours-scarecrow-police-shifts-2022-abandoning

#### Why SDI Is a Stronger Shock

Most event-based shocks (festivals, closures, one-off incidents) are narrow and
potentially confounded with concurrent demand changes. SDI is more useful
because it directly moves police visibility intensity and has clear timing:

1. SDI launch in February 2022.
2. SDI expanded in July 2022 to seven days and all watches.
3. SDI suspended effective February 1, 2024.

If SDI assignment intensity varies across districts/beats/watches, that creates
the first-stage variation needed to identify route-level safety costs.

---

#### Proposed Identification Design

Unit of analysis:
- Edge-time panel (street segment x hour/day) matched to Strava Metro edge
  counts and/or taxi flows.

Treatment:
- Patrol exposure on edge `e` at time `t`, constructed from SDI assignment data.
- Baseline proxy if needed: distance-weighted exposure from beat/district-level
  SDI staffing within the same watch.

Design:
1. Event study around Feb 2022, Jul 2022, and Feb 1, 2024.
2. Difference-in-differences comparing high-exposure vs low-exposure edges.
3. Triple-difference using watch intensity (e.g., night vs day) where SDI
   reallocations were stronger.

Estimand:
- Semi-elasticity of route/edge flow with respect to policing-induced safety
  exposure, mapped into the crime disutility parameter in a network discrete
  choice model.

Threats and mitigation:
1. Endogenous redeployment to hot spots:
   Use policy timing shocks and pre-period exposure shares; include edge and
   time fixed effects plus area-specific trends.
2. Concurrent downtown events:
   Control for event calendar and weather; run robustness excluding major-event
   dates/areas.
3. Measurement error in assignment geography:
   FOIA request should ask for beat/sector-level deployment and timestamps.

---

#### Data Requirements for the SDI Design

Minimum required fields:
1. Assignment date and start/end timestamps.
2. District, beat, and (if available) sector/geocode of assignment.
3. Unit type (marked patrol car, foot patrol, etc.).
4. Overtime flag and hours worked.
5. Program label indicating SDI vs regular patrol.
6. Any policy memos defining SDI operating rules over time.

Outcome data already in scope:
- Strava Metro edge counts by time bin.
- Chicago taxi trip flows (pickup/dropoff, timestamps).
- Chicago crime incidents (point/time), aggregated to route exposure measures.

#### Draft FOIA Request (Illinois FOIA)

Subject: Illinois FOIA Request - CPD SDI Patrol Assignment Records (2021-Present)

To the Chicago Police Department FOIA Officer:

Pursuant to the Illinois Freedom of Information Act (5 ILCS 140), I request
electronic records sufficient to identify Strategic Decision Initiative (SDI,
also referred to as "scarecrow") patrol assignments and related deployment
activity from January 1, 2021 through the present.

Please provide, for each SDI assignment/shift record:
1. Assignment date.
2. Start and end time (or start timestamp and duration).
3. District and beat (and sector, if available).
4. Assignment/program type indicating SDI vs non-SDI activity.
5. Unit type (e.g., marked patrol vehicle, foot patrol), if tracked.
6. Overtime indicator and overtime hours (if applicable).
7. Employee identifier in de-identified form (unique anonymized ID is
   sufficient; names and star numbers are not requested).

In addition, please provide:
1. Any data dictionary, codebook, or schema documentation needed to interpret
   these fields.
2. Any CPD directives, operations orders, or policy memos from 2021-present
   describing SDI launch, expansion, modification, and suspension timelines.
3. Aggregate beat-level patrol staffing summaries by watch/day, if individual
   assignment-level data cannot be released.

Format requested:
- Machine-readable electronic format (CSV, XLSX, or JSON for tabular data; PDF
  for policy documents).
- Please include all responsive records as maintained in the ordinary course of
  business.

If any records are withheld, please cite each claimed exemption with sufficient
specificity and release all reasonably segregable non-exempt portions.
