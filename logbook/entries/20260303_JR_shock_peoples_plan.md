### Shock Note - People's Plan Priority Block Groups (March 2024)

**Date:** 2026-03-03

This note documents the March 13, 2024 rollout of hyperlocal "priority block
groups" under Chicago's People's Plan for Community Safety as a candidate
region-specific shock.

---

#### Event Definition

On **March 13, 2024**, Mayor Brandon Johnson announced that anti-violence and
community-safety efforts would focus on **10 specific block groups** across
four neighborhoods: Englewood, West Garfield Park, Austin, and Little Village.

The public descriptions identify the treatment corridors as:
1. Englewood: between 59th and 63rd and along Garfield Blvd.
2. West Garfield Park: around Madison/Adams/Jackson near I-290.
3. Austin: around Madison/Adams/West End.
4. Little Village: around 26th and 27th near Pulaski.

---

#### Why It Could Identify Crime Avoidance

1. The intervention is explicitly hyperlocal and place-based.
2. Timing is sharp at announcement/launch date.
3. Geography can be coded from corridor descriptions and compared to nearby
   non-priority segments.

Primary design use:
- Event-study or DiD on edge-level flows inside vs outside priority blocks,
  with pre-trend checks and neighborhood-by-time controls.

Main limitation:
- The rollout is investment/service-oriented (not a single enforcement change),
  so treatment intensity may phase in and require implementation data.
